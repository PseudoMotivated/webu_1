Year Category Laureate Reason
1909 Literature Selma Lagerlöf For the noble idealism, richness of imagination, and soulful expression that characterize her writing.
1912 Physics Gustaf Dalén For his invention of self-operating regulators to be used in combination with gas accumulators for lighthouse and buoy illumination.
1921 Peace Hjalmar Branting For his work with the League of Nations and for international cooperation.
1930 Peace Nathan Söderblom As a church leader, he sought to create a common Christian platform for peace.
1948 Chemistry Arne Tiselius For his research on electrophoresis.
1951 Literature Pär Lagerkvist For the artistic strength and true independence of mind he seeks in his poetry to find answers to the eternal questions faced by humanity.
1961 Peace Dag Hammarskjöld For building an effective and independent UN Secretariat and taking an independent stance towards the great powers.
1970 Physiology or Medicine Ulf von Euler For discoveries regarding neurotransmitters in nerve terminals and the mechanisms of their storage, release, and inactivation.
1974 Economics Gunnar Myrdal For his work in monetary and business cycle theory and studies on the relationship between economic, social, and institutional conditions.
1974 Literature Harry Martinson For a body of work that captures the dewdrop and reflects the cosmos.
1982 Peace Alva Myrdal As a representative of a non-aligned Sweden, she actively worked to persuade the superpowers to disarm.
2015 Chemistry Tomas Lindahl For mechanistic studies of DNA repair.
2023 Physics Anne L'Huillier For experimental methods that generate attosecond light pulses for studies of electron dynamics in matter.
